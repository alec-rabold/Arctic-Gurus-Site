5 Key Things You Must Do Before Launch

1. Perfect your marketing message

 You’re eager to get your product out there and start pulling in paychecks, but if your message isn’t clear, you customers won’t buy. Make sure your headlines support the product’s primary benefits. Does your product make the process faster or easier? Does it simplify a complex process? Does it have a toolbox of helpful features? If you don’t present your message clearly, your clients won’t buy in and you’ve missed your opportunity.



2. Streamline your checkout process

 Having a quick, simple way for customers to select your products and pay sounds easy enough, however it’s one of the top reasons why products don’t sell. If your checkout system has flaws or is difficult to navigate, it’s going to subconciously reflect the quality of your product. Make it easy! You need to make sure the checkout process is simple and that it works flawlessly.



3. Plan your advertising and PR

 Are you buying an ad in a newsletter? Are you writing an article to submit to websites? It’s okay to start small if your budget is limited, but you have to get the word out about your product and build buzz before you launch.



4. Explain your product to your employees

 As obvious as it sounds, it’s essential and often overlooked. Don’t assume your employees will know what you have in mind for the product unless you explicitly tell them. If they don’t understand the marketing pitch, then there’s no way they can sell it. Meet with your employees and discuss your product so they can convert leads into sales.



5. Reward your current customers

 A great way to show that you value your loyal customers is by sending them discounts. They deserve special treatment for being special to your business. They are predisposed to buying from you and will provide free word-of-mouth marketing. Send them an email or special newsletter with offers tailored to their interests. Make sure you track the results to improve this method of conversion for the future.




 The key to thinking like a successful businessman is presenting your marketing message as the answer to your customer’s problems. Assume that your product has value and will enhance your prospect’s life - and explain this to them. If you don’t believe your product has value yourself, then you won’t be able to sustain the message to your clients.
