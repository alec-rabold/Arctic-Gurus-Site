Find out why customers like you

 A marketing program should be based on a marketing strategy, which is the big-picture idea driving your success. The program shows each coordinated activity that together make up the tactics. Put simply, your program is the way you want to implement your strategy. You don’t have to dive into the technicalities of strategies and plans just yet, because this chapter will cover lots of simpler, quicker things you can do to leverage your marketing activities.



Know your customer

 The first and most critical principle of marketing is: Know your customer. Once you understand how your customers think and what they like, you can find appropriate and appealing ways to communicate with them about the products you want them to buy.

 You can’t be all things to all customers. You can’t be the best on every rational and emotional decision. If you try to meet with competition on their own ground, you won’t get past second place. So now I want you to clearly and succinctly define what your special strength or advantage is. Start with: “My product is special because…”

 Your answer to that question reflects whatever the brilliance of your product or business is. Marketing agency director, Cecilia Rocks, coined the concept brilliance marketing, working with marketers to identify their most fundamental strengths before trying any advertising, sales, publicity, etc. Then she makes sure that everything they move forward with is based on their strengths.

 I urge you to use this strength-based marketing method to add an extra degree of focus to your personalized marketing program. Take a minute to remind yourself of what makes your business special. Check out why your business isn’t creative to help spark your imagination. Make sure your program amplifies and reflects your light and never loses sight of it.

 For example, if your company is known for quality customer service, make sure to train, recognize, and reward good service in your employees, and to emphasize good service in all communications with your customers. Perhaps you would feature a photo of a friendly, helpful employee in your brochures or web page. Focus on your strength in all that you do, and your marketing programs will become more profitable.

The best way to find customers

 Every so often I survey managers of successful businesses to ask them about their marketing practices. And the first and most important question I ask is, “What is the best way to attract customers?” Now, the interesting thing about this question is that the answer differs for every successful business. Therefore, you need to answer this question yourself; you’re not going to find the answer in a guide.

 Take a look at the following list so get some ideas of the most common answers - things that businesses often say are most effective at bringing them customers:

Referrals:
Customers sell the product through effective word of mouth marketing (HYPERLINK)
Trade Shows/Professional Meetings:
Contacts sell the product
Sales Calls:
Salespeople sell the product (ultimate guide to cold-calling)

Product Demonstrations, Trial Coupons, Free Samples: Product sells itself (how to sit back and let your product sell itself)

Web Sites and Newsletters: Internet information and display sells the product (most effective, least work websites)
Placement and Appearance of Stores:
Location sells the product

 Every business has a unique optimal formula for attracting customers. However, in every case, successful businesses report that one or two methods work best. This means that their marketing programs are dominated by one or two of these techniques. They put ⅓ to ⅔ of their marketing resources into the top ways of attracting customers, and then use the other methods to support and leverage their most effective method.

 So, you need to find the one best way to attract customers to your business. What is the best way to attract customers, and how can you focus your marketing program to take fuller advantage of it?

 Once you answer this question, you’re taking another important step toward a highly focused marketing program that leverages your resources as much as possible.

 Your program can likely be divided into the following three categories:

Works best
Helpful
Doesn't work

 If you reorganize last year’s budget into these categories, you may well find that your spending isn’t concentrated near the top of your list. If not, then you can move your focus and spending up. We call this the marketing pyramid, and we challenge marketers to move their spending up the pyramid so their resources are concentrated near the top. What does your marketing pyramid look like? Can you move it up by shifting resources and investments to higher-pulling marketing activities?
