How is this series organized?

 The purpose of this series is to provide you with a practical and proven, step-by-step guide for preparing your own marketing plan. We include real-world answers to help you meet specific marketing challenges head on, whatever your marketing expertise or size/type of your organization. We stand by our guide as a very useful resource that will help you find marketing solutions on an everyday basis.

Our free guide is organized into the following sections:

Part I:
Designing a Great Marketing Program

Part II:
Leveraging Your Marketing Skills

Part III:
Advertising for Fun and Profit

Part IV:
Powerful Alternatives to Advertising

Part V:
Sell or be Sold; Selling Great Products to Anyone, Anytime, Anywhere


My Naive Assumptions

 I assume you’re very reasonably intelligent (that parts not a foolish assumption, based on what I know about my audience). You’re highly motivated to be seeking out new ideas and I assume that you’re willing to try new ideas that could improve your sales. Marketing is challenging because requires an open mind and a willingness to experiment. Although I believe you are intelligent, I assume that you don’t have all the technical knowledge you may need to succeed in marketing, so I explain each concept as clearly as I can.

 I assume that you’re able to be imaginative and creative at one moment, then switch to being analytical and rigorous the next. Sometimes, I ask you to run your numbers and make sales projections. Other times, I ask you to dream up a clever way to captivate your reader’s eye and communicate a benefit to them. These demands pull you from side to side. I you can assemble a team of varied, skillful people, then you’re marketing campaign will come more easily. Though if you are a small business owner, you’re very likely all you have - and you have to wear many job descriptions at the same time. But hey, at least you won’t get bored as you wrestle the challenges of marketing!

 Finally, I assume that you have an active interest in generating new sales and expanding your satisfaction of existing customers. Your persistence and sales orientation needs to support everything you do in marketing. Remember that the broader purpose of this series is to help you make more sales happen!



Our Beliefs

 We understand that there a lot of books that provide examples of what others have done. What’s really needed, and what we provide, is a guide with a how-to format that helps and directs you in creative effective marketing. We continue to believe in and practice marketing that links its three disciplines: research, strategy, and tactical execution. While the vast majority of this guide is structured on hard factual data, we’ve continued to include personal experiences, observations, and opinions. For these we take full responsibility; and likewise, any errors are our own.



Where to go from here?

 If you read only one section of this tutorial, please let it be Part I of this series. We’ve designed this chapter with the sole purpose of auditing your marketing and enhancing the things you do to make profitable sales. The rest of series is packed full of quality tips and techniques, and it all deserves attention. But whatever else you do or don’t get around to, read the first chapter with a pen and paper at hand!
