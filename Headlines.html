Headlines are Ride-High or Die

 Want to grab your readers’ attention? Breaking News: Headlines Are Critically Important. Staticians quote that 8 out of 10 people will read the headline, but only 2 out of ten will read more than that. That means it's critical to get the headline right. They also found that most people only scan online content. This is important - it means you have three seconds to grab your readers’ attention and make them want to read further.



5 Types of Effective Headlines

1. Questions -

 Ask people something you know they want to answer to. (Ex. “Who wants to earn more and work less”).


2. Problem-Based -

 Figure out what crucial business problem your product can solve. (Ex. “Is your marketing strategy is missing a vital link?”)


3. Curiosity -

 Use a term that people may not know the definition of, but that they can see the importance of. (Ex. “Are your products missing the wow factor?”)


4. How-To -

 Tell them how to do something you know they want to do. (Ex. “How to reduce your weight without exercise”).


5. Testimonials -

 Have other people tell you audience how great your products and services are. (Ex. “Thomas Jones is a miracle worker. We made an extra $100,000 in one week by following his advice”).



 Do these headlines sound a little clickbait-esque? That’s okay. Clickbait titles got their nickname from their powerful ability to draw readers in. This is especially common in blogs. Bloggers must make powerful titles time after time again to keep their usual readers interested while capturing new audiences. Your headlines need to be catchy and suggest that if they read further, they will be rewarded.

 There’s a few key components to writing an engaging headline. First, your headline mst promise something to the reader. This promise should later be fulfilled in the article. Next, when you are writing a headline, be aware of your keywords. Search engines will find your articles more easily if they are optimized to specific words. The key is to do this without sacrificing a powerful headline. Finally, great headlines don’t need to be created from scratch. Research various successful titles and you’ll soon get a feel for what’s common in a captivating headline.



The "So-What?" Factor



 If you want to get your reader's’ attention then you must put the benefits of your product/service in the headline. My clients often have long lists of features that they want to present their customers, so here’s a trick to help focus the list:

Before: “Our software has built-in upgrades.”
After: “Our software has built-in upgrades (SO) you never have to buy another copy of the product, saving you both time and money.”

Before: “Our plant food contains three special growth minerals.”
After: “Our plant food contains three special growth minerals (SO) your plants grow to twice the size in half the work!”

 The idea is fairly straightforward: figure out your product’s benefit then determine the impact on your customers. It’s important that you create the “So-What” in terms of your customer’s perspective - put yourself in their shoes. What specific reason would make YOU buy your own product?



The Interest Factor



 After you build a magnetic headline, you’ve got the reader’s attention. Next, you need to create interest in your product. Numerous studies of human behaviour and psychology declare that the best way to do this is with anecdotes supported by facts. An anecdote is a personal story that often allows your reader to relate to the content in some way. You want your reader able to answer his subconscious “What’s in it for me?” question as soon as possible. Therefore it’s imperative that you focus on the specific interests of your prospects - not your own company’s. Don’t tell your customers how your company is the first to create a comb with rubber tips - instead tell them how the comb will take their hairstyle to the next level in half the time and twice the performance. There is a place for your company’s personal story, but it’s not in your sales pitch.
