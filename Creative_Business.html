Why your business doesn't stand out

 Intelligent business owners ask why creativity is such an important factor when dealing with web marketing. It's simple if you think about the big picture - we live in a world that treasures unique, conceptual thinking. Put yourself in your customer's shoes. If your business hardly interests yourself, then you've already lost.

 There's this myth that says creativity comes naturally. In reality, that's not the case. As we grow up our creativity is constantly discouraged, most significantly through school where your value is judged by your conformity. In the real world, conformity is a 9 to 5 job; innovation is forging your own path. Let’s look at how you will be prevented from reaching your business goals if you do not encourage creativity.

 Resistance is the only thing preventing your from reaching your goals. It comes in many forms: fear, envy, negativity, even “helpful” comments from your own friends. Essentially, to overcome resistance, you not only have to listen to yourself - you have to follow your own voice. I’m confident that you can come up with at least five reasons why you shouldn’t try a new business idea or approach. Most of them are probably reasonable - even though they might not be entirely true, they’re reasonable. Now recognize they're external voices of resistance. You must believe you know what’s best for your company. Remember that your company wouldn't have left the drawing boards if you didn't design it. Creativity will help you accomplish this.



How Does Creativity Benefit Your Business?

 You own a small business, so you already have a vantage point to larger, more established businesses. That is, you will respond to the ever-changing market quickly and efficiently. Now here's where you put your creativity to practice. You need to be ready for the next big product or service. In an interview with CIO Magazine, world-famous author and innovator Geoffrey Moore quotes, “If you allocate all your resources based on your revenue makers or margin makers, you’re driving in the rearview mirror.” You must be ready for the new wave of innovation. Develop and channel your inner creativity:
 

Creativity allows you to:


 Set your company vision and direction

 Deal with changing markets

 Find new niches and products

 Increase productivity

 Balance your work and home life


7 Steps to Uncovering Your Creativity

1. Read about different topics simultaneously

 Knowledge is a gift. Yes that sounds cliche, but it’s not just bookworms who understand this fact. When futurist Alvin Toffler was interviewed on C-Span’s Book TV about his book Revolutionary Wealth, he talked about the specific techniques he uses to foster his own creativity and subsequent success:

  Like most creative people, he is a voracious reader. However, he puts a spin on this technique. He reads TONS of books and articles simultaneously. And as a result, he sees connections that he would’ve otherwise missed: information about history, news stories, current web trends, points of view, etc. Taking all this knowledge in concurrently gives him a unique perspective on the world as one; and hence, paves an assortment of ideas into a single effective marketing campaign.



2. Make things as simple as possible

 When people get too excited about showing off their knowledge, they tend to present something really complex. You’ve probably noticed this. It often includes way too many PowerPoint slides and not enough clarity. When you boil ideas down to their essence, you separate your pride from your ego and become an effective teacher.

 In December of 2006, Google Founders Sergey Brin and Larry Page discussed how they succeeded with simplicity as a virtue. They created a search engine that operates with straightforward keywords. What could be simpler than that? These pioneers of the digital age agree that simplicity is “an important trend to be focused on.” Take an idea, grow it, and shave off the excess.



3. Spend time on the design

 In this day in age, design matters. It’s the language of ideas we use to communicate our beliefs and dreams. Spend time designing a well-thought-out website and graphics and it will go a long way toward showing your customer you pay attention to detail.

 At the same time, you don’t need to go overboard with your design. Simply add an ornament to demonstrate that your company will go the extra mile. For example, Nordstrom instructs their sales clerks to physically hand customers their items after a sale. That’s how they show they care about their customer and personalize the encounter. Think about your own sales process and brainstorm what you can do to make a difference to the customer.



4. Keep a notebook for your 'stages'

 Document everything you create with this interesting twist: record each idea in different stages of development. Keep a notebook for you early ideas, one for those being shaped, and one for your final ideas. Let your ideas incubate. Leave an idea in your early stages notebook and come back to it in a few days, adding fresh content and ideas to nurture your perspective since the last look.



5. Keep your focus sharp

 Whenever I’m in the middle of a big project, I remind myself of a core principle developed by Rosabeth Moss Kanter of Harvard Business School. She tells us that “all projects look like a disaster in the middle.” Creativity requires you to occasionally lose control. When you are amidst your next project, remember that this stage is essential to creating a new process. As Winston Churchill puts it, “If you are going through Hell, keep going.”



6. Collect inspiration and use it

 Over the years, I have found a lot of inspirational quotes, articles, and books. I pull them out whenever I’m feeling like I need to give my creativity a boost. The result is astonishing. These inspirations are often powerful enough to change your mood, subsequently heightening your level of creativity.



7. Stay in the "know"

 Disclaimer: this doesn’t mean spending all your downtime binge-watching that hit new show your friends can’t stop talking about. Though, you should be aware of your customers’ interests - which are often intertwined with popular culture. The key to incorporating this with business is asking, “What’s new and different today?” Keep up on new ideas and cultural trends. Staying aware of the fast-paced present will prevent your from hurting yourself with an outdated marketing strategy and no customers.
